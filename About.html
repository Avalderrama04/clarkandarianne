Indian Ladder Farms

A fun you-pick apple orchard and restaurant and brewery. They brew their own cider with their own apples, beer with their own hops, and cook delicious food with produce from the farm. If the timing is right, there just might be apple cider donuts! It’s fun for the whole family! Situated below the Helderberg Escarpment, it might provide a refreshing finish to a hike at Thatcher Park.

Thatcher Park

One of our favorite parks in the Capital Region! Its location straddling the Helderberg Escarpment gives impressive views of the surrounding hills and valleys. Many hiking trails provide cool up-close encounters with unique New York geology with striking waterfalls over shale formations.

Lisha Kill

A small nature preserve near Clark’s parents house that Clark and Ari love. It has a few miles of trails by a beautiful brook and its ravine. It provides an escape from surrounding cars and noises of modern life at all times of year. Some of our favorite memories are visiting after a thick snowfall.

Albany Pine Brush

One of the largest remaining intact tracts of Atlantic Coastal Pine Barrens left in the world! Clark worked here for a summer and loved it! There are many walking trails open to the public and a large Discovery Center for people of all ages to learn about the unique ecosystem that is the Pine Bush.

Schenectady Greenmarket

The second best farmers market ever! Open every Sunday (including the one after the wedding) in downtown Schenectady from 10am-2pm, this market would give you an accurate taste of the bounty of the surrounding area’s farms and food producers.

Frog Alley Brewing Company

if you like to drink beer, this brewery is our favorite in the Capital Region. It opened a few years ago and has been a staple in the Schenectady live music scene and community gathering space since then. While you’re there, you can order pizza at the bar (when in New York, do as the New Yorkers do).

Historic Downtown Troy

Just upriver from Albany, the Collar City is full of cool architecture from the early 20th century. The Troy Savings Bank Music Hall, in particular, is beautiful inside and out and has some of the best acoustics of any concert hall in the world. Plenty of shops, restaurants, and nice walking exists on the roads off of Riverfront Park.

Washington Park Neighborhood of Albany

Central Ave and Lark St all have plenty of restaurants, cafes, bars, and shops to enjoy. Washington Park itself will be beautiful in early September!
